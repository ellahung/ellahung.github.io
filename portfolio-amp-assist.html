One‑day UXathon case study

Amp Assist – Unified EV Support Experience

Designing a more coherent AI‑enabled support experience that connects charge point operators and EV drivers.

Award: Best Client Relationship – UXathon 2025

Duration
1 day (UXathon)
Team
4 UX / UI designers + mentor
My role
Client communication & problem framing
Project overview

From fragmented tools to a cohesive support journey

Amp Assist is an AI‑native BPO working with charge point operators (CPOs) to monitor EV charging networks and support drivers when charging sessions fail. Their two products – Amp Pulse for operations and Amp Nexus for customer support – had grown quickly, but the experience felt fragmented for both teams and drivers.

In a one‑day UXathon, our team was asked to imagine how these tools could feel like one unified service. We focused on connecting back‑office monitoring with frontline driver support, so that failed sessions could be detected, resolved and learned from more seamlessly.

Team & My role

Keeping the client and team aligned

Our team of four worked with a remote client and mentor in a compressed timeframe, balancing ambition with what we could realistically prototype in one day. We needed a clear problem frame and fast feedback loops to make the most of the UXathon.

  • Set up a shared question log so we could continuously clarify the brief and document decisions with the client.
  • Helped articulate a B2B2C framing that linked CPO operations and EV driver support into one journey.
  • Turned the refined problem statement into prioritised flows for the dashboard and call log views.
  • Co‑presented the final concept and how it could fit into Amp Assist’s existing products.

Our team received the Best Client Relationship award for the way we managed expectations and kept communication open throughout the sprint.

Process

How we used the UXathon day

With one day and a pre‑reading window, we combined async questions, mentoring and rapid prototyping to move from a broad B2B2C brief to a focused concept around dashboards and call intelligence.

Before the day
Clarifying the brief

Ahead of the UXathon, I opened a shared question document and Slack thread, and set up short online check‑ins to capture what we did not understand about the two products, workflows and success metrics. This helped us align with the client that, for this sprint, we would focus on CPO‑facing tooling rather than driver apps.

Morning
Framing a B2B2C journey

With our mentor, we mapped how an incident travels from the charger to operations and then to the driver. This B2B2C view helped us see where Pulse and Nexus overlapped, and where a unified dashboard and call log could reduce hand‑offs.

Afternoon
Prototyping key screens

Once the scope was clear, we split work across a unified service dashboard and an AI‑augmented call log. While teammates focused on UI, I kept checking flows against the agreed problem statement and collected last‑minute feedback from the client.

Key interventions

What we prototyped in a day

We focused on evolving Amp Assist’s existing web console with a few targeted changes, rather than redesigning the platform from scratch.

01
Unifying Pulse and Nexus in one web dashboard

We combined operational KPIs and support metrics into a single overview page, so CPO teams can monitor charger health, incident volume and AI performance without jumping between separate tools.

02
Turning the call log into an intelligence view

On the calls page, we introduced a persistent filter bar and clearer grouping by outcome, topic and sentiment, so support leads can quickly answer questions like “Which errors create the most frustrated drivers?” directly from the web console.

Outcome

What we delivered in the UXathon

We handed over a clickable prototype, journey sketches and a short presentation that showed how a unified dashboard and call experience could make Amp Assist’s BPO feel more cohesive for CPOs.

  • Aligned with the client on a realistic scope and a B2B2C framing before the UXathon day.
  • Produced key flows and UI for a unified dashboard and call log within the one‑day sprint.
  • Received the Best Client Relationship award from the organisers.
What I learned

Practising fast, relationship‑driven design

This project was a good reminder that even in a hackathon, communication can be as important as pixels.

  • How to use a simple question log to keep clients, mentors and teammates aligned under time pressure.
  • How a clear B2B2C framing can keep UI decisions grounded in the end‑to‑end service.
  • How to prioritise a few high‑impact interventions instead of trying to redesign an entire platform in a day.
